Jaguars QB Trevor Lawrence, wife Marissa celebrate five years together

The milestones keep on coming for rookie Jacksonville Jaguars quarterback Trevor Lawrence and wife Marissa Mowry.

On Sunday, the high-school sweethearts-turned-newlyweds celebrated five years together, one month after tying the knot in South Carolina.

“5 years with you,” Mowry gushed on Instagram, sharing an intimate photo of the couple from their wedding day taken by photographer Katie Wilson.

Lawrence, who was selected first overall by the Jaguars in last month’s 2021 NFL Draft, also called Mowry “the realest” in a separate post, adding he’s “thankful.”

Mowry, who attended Anderson University in South Carolina, got engaged to Lawrence in July 2020. The former Clemson Tigers quarterback popped the question at Clemson’s Memorial Stadium in South Carolina.

“FOREVER,” the 6-foot-6 athlete posted to Instagram at the time. “You’re everything I’ve dreamed of. I love you and can’t wait to marry you.”

Mowry and Lawrence, both 21, swapped vows in early April. Ahead of the draft, Jaguars fans showered the new Mr. and Mrs. with wedding gifts.

“Thank you Jaguars fans. We really appreciate the wedding gifts and donations for charities of our choice! In addition to the donations, Marissa and I will also be donating $20K to charities in Jacksonville,” Lawrence tweeted at the time.

When the Jaguars drafted Lawrence with the No. 1 pick, Mowry was by her husband’s side to celebrate.

“So excited to be a JAG!!” Mowry gushed on Instagram.

Last week, Jaguars brass revealed they had trouble getting in contact with Lawrence on draft night, noting his phone sent the team to voicemail three times.

Fortunately, the connection wasn’t totally lost and Jacksonville landed their quarterback of the future.
