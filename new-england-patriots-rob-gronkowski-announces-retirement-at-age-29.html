New England Patriots Rob Gronkowski Announces Retirement At Age 29

It is the matter of the surprise that the ‘Grateful’ Rob Gronkowski finished his NFL career in an early age. However, the New England Patriots’s dominant tight end did not disclose the reason behind his retirement.

American football tight end Rob Gronkowski announced his retirement from the NFL from 24th March 2019. The 29-year old played the entire career with the New England Patriots, thanked his teammates and fans through an Instagram post.

However, he did not disclose the reason behind his retirement. Reports suggest that he agreed to a deal with a film or television company for the upcoming Super Bowl.

The Patriots coach Bill Belichick said it was his pleasure and a privilege to coach Rob being nine years together, and credited him as a major reason the Patriots won championships.

Rob began his career after he was drafted by the Patriots in the second round (42nd overall) of the 2010 NFL Draft. The freshman played nine seasons for the team and has the most seasons with 1,000+ receiving yards by a tight end with four, and also the most career postseason receiving yards by a tight end.

He played in 115 regular-season games, a total of 521 catches for 7,861 yards. Rob was named to the Pro Bowl five times and became first-team All-Pro four times. He recently signed a six-year, $54 million contract with the New England Patriots in January 2019. Rob has boasted the significant amount of net worth, estimated to $15 million from his NFL career.

Rob Gronkowski Bio; Parents, Girlfriend, Height, Weight

The former NFL star was born as Robert James Gronkowski on 14 May 1989 to parents Gordon Gronkowski and Diane Walters. His father Gordon was college football guard for Syracuse University. He grew up along with four brothers Gordie, Dan, Chris, and Glenn, who are also a professional footballer.

Rob attended Williamsville North High School for three years and later at Woodland Hills High School in Churchill. After graduation, he went to the University of Arizona as a Pre-Business major. The power pack has a towering height of 6 feet 6 inches (1.98 meters) and a weight of 120 kg (268 pounds).
